Solid Waste Sorting And Recycling Automation

Urbanization, economic prosperity, and population growth have led to a sharp increase in consumption and waste generation.

Municipal Solid Waste Composition

In India, material consumption has risen six-fold from 1970 to 2015 and is projected to double by 2030. The urban population is also expected to grow significantly. As a result, India will generate massive amounts of waste, posing operational challenges that require substantial resources to manage it effectively.

Around 45 per cent of Municipal Solid waste comprises food waste and biodegradables. Approximately 75,000 TPD of wet waste is generated daily, which is increasing due to population growth and changing lifestyles. Government data shows a significant improvement in damp waste processing, rising from 18% in 2014 to 68% in 2021. Critical key interventions such as composting, biomethanisation, and waste-to-energy are being promoted to address municipal wet waste.

Dry waste, accounting for approximately 35% of total waste, is a significant concern. Plastic waste generation, at a rate of 2,600 tons per day, poses a significant challenge in waste management. Plastic waste generation in India is predicted to increase more than threefold from current levels, making us the fifth-largest contributor to marine litter by 2025.

Smart Waste Sorting and Recycling Automation Using IoT Devices

Smart waste sorting and recycling automation is an innovative approach that leverages Internet of Things (IoT) technology to enhance waste management processes. By integrating IoT devices, sensors, and data analytics, municipalities and organizations can optimize waste collection, improve recycling rates, and promote sustainable practices. This system not only streamlines operations but also provides valuable insights into waste generation patterns

Key Components of Smart Waste Sorting and Recycling Automation

IoT-Enabled Waste Bins:


Smart Sensors:
Equipped with fill-level sensors, these bins monitor the amount of waste they contain in real-time. This data helps in optimizing collection routes and schedules, reducing unnecessary trips and operational costs.
Waste Type Identification:
Some smart bins can identify the type of waste deposited (e.g., plastic, glass, organic) using sensors and AI algorithms, ensuring proper sorting at the source.

Data Collection and Transmission:


Real-Time Monitoring: IoT devices continuously collect data on waste levels, types, and collection times. This information is transmitted to a central cloud-based platform for analysis.
Cloud Integration: Data from various bins is aggregated in the cloud, allowing for real-time monitoring and management of waste collection operations.

Automated Sorting Systems:


AI and Machine Learning: Advanced algorithms analyze the data collected from smart bins to classify waste types accurately. This classification can be done using image recognition technologies that identify materials based on visual characteristics.
Robotic Sorting: In recycling facilities, robotic arms equipped with sensors can automatically sort waste into different categories based on the AI classification results, improving efficiency and accuracy.

Web Dashboard and Analytics:


User -Friendly Interface: A web-based dashboard provides waste management authorities with real-time insights into waste levels, collection schedules, and recycling rates. This interface allows for easy monitoring and decision-making.
Data Analytics: The system analyzes historical data to identify trends in waste generation, helping municipalities plan for future needs and implement targeted recycling programs.

Alerts and Notifications:


Automated Alerts: When bins reach a certain fill level (e.g., 90%), the system can automatically send alerts to waste collection teams, ensuring timely pickups and preventing overflow.
User Engagement: Mobile applications can notify residents about collection schedules, recycling guidelines, and community engagement initiatives, promoting responsible waste disposal practices.
